Ada Lovelace


1815 1852 London, England British Died in London Homeschooling she was privately educated in mathematics and science Mathematician (many awards, medals and a programming language are named after her) The first computer programmer – She developed a computer programming system (algorithm) without computers being invented yet most known for her work on Charles Babbage's proposed mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine. 19th Century, Pioneer, Mathematician

Already at a young age, Ada was very curious, talented and interested in science. At the age of 12 she studied birds over years and wanted to invent a flying machine.

Due to the fact that women in the 19th century did not have the same rights as men and were not allowed to enter universities or academic libraries, her husband William King helped her by copying articles in the library."
