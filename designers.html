Ada Lovelace

Ada Lovelace lived in the 19th century and was quite ahead of her time: She developed a computer programming system without computers being invented yet. Back then, even in wealthy families like Ada’s, it was not common for women to get the same education as men – so Ada was very lucky that her mother provided her the best homeschooling teachers. Especially Charles Babbage had a huge influence on her: He had been working on a mechanical calculating machine and since Ada understood his quite complex thoughts, they started working together. Ada was only 36 when she died of cancer in 1852. In the 1970s, a computer language (which is still used today) was named after her: It’s called ADA.

1: Already at a young age, Ada was very curious, talented and interested in science. At the age of 12 she studied birds over years and wanted to invent a flying machine. 2: Due to the fact that women in the 19th century did not have the same rights as men and were not allowed to enter universities or academic libraries, her husband William King helped her by copying articles in the library."
